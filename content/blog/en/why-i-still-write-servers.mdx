The industry pendulum keeps swinging. A few years ago everything had to be serverless. Now edge runtimes are the default. Tomorrow it'll be something else.

Here's my unfashionable opinion: for most products, a boring server running Node or Go on a VPS is still an excellent choice. Let me explain why.

## Cold starts are a tax

Every serverless abstraction leaks cold start latency somewhere. You can minimize it — provisioned concurrency, keep-warm tricks — but you can't eliminate it, and paying for warm containers is a server with extra steps.

## Debugging is real

When a serverless function misbehaves, your debugging tools are the provider's logs and traces. When a server misbehaves, you can SSH in, attach a profiler, strace a syscall. The latter is slower but more powerful. Senior engineers know that production debugging is where experience pays off.

## Cost at scale

For steady workloads, a VPS costs a fraction of equivalent serverless. I've seen teams cut 70% of their infra bill by moving off Lambda once they understood their actual load profile.

## When serverless wins

Serverless is still the right call for:

- Event-driven workloads with unpredictable spikes
- Teams that don't want to own any infra at all
- Edge-critical latency where global distribution matters more than compute cost

The mistake is making it the default. Start boring. Optimize when you have evidence.
